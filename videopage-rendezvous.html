Rendez-Vous - Short Film (NL)

Rendez-Vous is a shortfilm directed by Anéline Plateau.

The movie follows the story of an elderly woman named Julia, who is struggling to cope with the loss of her husband. Desperate for human interaction, she starts visiting the dentist's office every day and spends hours sitting in the waiting room, observing people. Over time, Margaret becomes a familiar face to the staff and patients at the dentist's office. She strikes up conversations with other patients, who come to appreciate her kindness and wise words.


Through her visits to the dentist's office, Julia learns to open up about her grief and begins to find joy in the simple pleasures of life. She discovers that while she may never fully get over the loss of her husband, she can still find purpose and meaning in connecting with others and helping them through their own struggles. Her newfound friendships at the dentist's office, combined with the companionship of her pet bunny at home, bring her solace in unexpected ways.


"Rendez-Vous" is a touching and heartwarming story about finding comfort in unexpected places and the power of human connection.
